What is NEET 2027?

NEET UG (National Eligibility cum Entrance Test) is India's single national-level entrance exam for undergraduate medical and dental courses (MBBS, BDS) at government and private colleges across India. Conducted by NTA, NEET 2027 will follow the established pattern of 180 questions across Biology, Physics and Chemistry for a total of 720 marks.

With over 20 lakh candidates competing for limited MBBS seats every year, NEET preparation demands exceptional strategy, consistency and regular practice. This guide breaks down everything you need to build your NEET 2027 preparation plan.

NEET 2027 Exam Pattern

  • Total Questions: 180 (Section A: 35 Qs + Section B: 15 Qs per subject — attempt any 10 from B)
  • Subjects: Physics (45Q), Chemistry (45Q), Botany (45Q), Zoology (45Q)
  • Total Marks: 720
  • Duration: 3 hours 20 minutes
  • Marking: +4 for correct, −1 for wrong
  • Mode: Offline (Pen and Paper)

🔬 Key Fact: Biology (Botany + Zoology) carries 360 out of 720 marks — that is 50% of the paper. Mastering Biology is the single biggest lever for NEET score improvement.

Subject-Wise NEET 2027 Strategy

Biology Strategy (360 Marks)

Biology is the most scoring section in NEET. The entire NEET Biology syllabus is NCERT-based. Read NCERT Class 11 and 12 Biology line by line — diagrams, definitions, exceptions, all of it. Students who master NCERT Biology consistently score 300+ in this section.

  • Read NCERT Biology at least 3 times cover to cover
  • Highlight exceptions, facts and statistical data in NCERT
  • Revise diagrams regularly — NEET asks diagram-based questions every year
  • Practice previous year NEET Biology PYQs topic-wise
  • High-priority chapters: Human Physiology, Genetics, Ecology, Plant Physiology

Physics Strategy (180 Marks)

NEET Physics tests your ability to apply formulas in different scenarios. Focus on Class 11 topics (Mechanics, Thermodynamics, Waves) and Class 12 topics (Electrostatics, Current Electricity, Modern Physics).

  • Understand concepts before memorising formulas
  • Practice numerical problems daily — at least 10–15 per day
  • High-priority: Mechanics, Electrostatics, Modern Physics, Optics
  • NCERT Physics is sufficient for conceptual questions; use DC Pandey for numericals

Chemistry Strategy (180 Marks)

NEET Chemistry is divided into Physical, Organic and Inorganic. Inorganic is memory-heavy but very scoring — many students score 50–55/60 in Chemistry with proper NCERT revision.

  • Physical Chemistry: Practice numericals daily — Mole concept, Equilibrium, Electrochemistry
  • Organic Chemistry: Learn named reactions, mechanisms and conversions
  • Inorganic Chemistry: Revise NCERT tables, properties and exceptions regularly
  • High-priority: Coordination Compounds, Biomolecules, Polymers, p-block elements

12-Month NEET 2027 Study Plan

Months 1–4: Foundation Phase

Cover NCERT thoroughly for all three subjects. Start with Biology — spend 60% of your time on Biology during this phase. Build solid concepts in Physics and Chemistry from NCERT before moving to reference books.

Months 5–8: Advanced Practice Phase

Start solving topic-wise PYQs and practice question banks. Take subject-wise mock tests weekly and review your AI analysis results to identify weak areas. Revise NCERT Biology weekly.

Months 9–12: Intensive Revision + Mocks Phase

Shift 70% of your time to full-length mock tests and revision. Target 3–4 full mocks per week. Use AI-powered analysis after every mock to pinpoint weak topics. Dedicate the last 4 weeks entirely to rapid NCERT revision and solving previous 10 years of NEET papers.

📊 Study Grid Prep Tip: Students who take 30+ full-length NEET mocks and use AI analysis consistently improve their scores by 60–80 marks in the last 3 months of preparation.

Time Management for NEET Preparation

A consistent 6–8 hour daily study routine is the key to NEET success. Here is a recommended daily schedule:

  • Morning (2.5 hrs): Biology — NCERT reading + PYQ practice
  • Afternoon (2 hrs): Physics concepts + 15 numericals
  • Evening (2 hrs): Chemistry — topic-wise practice
  • Night (1 hr): Mock test review / Inorganic Chemistry revision

Use a Focus Timer with Pomodoro sessions to maintain deep concentration. Live study rooms on Study Grid Prep help you stay accountable alongside thousands of other NEET aspirants.

Common NEET Preparation Mistakes

  • Not reading NCERT Biology thoroughly — it is the single most important resource
  • Skipping Inorganic Chemistry revision — it is very scoring in NEET
  • Taking too few mock tests — aim for 30+ full-length mocks
  • Not reviewing wrong answers after every mock test
  • Neglecting Physics numericals — they are solvable with practice
  • Burning out from too many study hours without proper breaks
